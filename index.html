Save Black Mothers

Black women compared to women of other racial or ethnic groups during pregnancy, childbirth, or within a defined period after pregnancy have higher incidents of maternal deaths. This can be due to a number of reasons, like historical and contemporary policies, practices, and norms that systematically disadvantage certain racial or ethnic groups. Black women face systemic barriers in areas such as education, employment, housing, and healthcare, which can negatively impact their health outcomes.

About Info session

Welcome to this session where we explore the unique challenges faced by expectant mothers from minority communities during pregnancy. We'll discuss factors such as socioeconomic disparities, access to healthcare, and systemic inequalities. Our goal is to foster understanding and empower advocacy for equitable healthcare practices.
